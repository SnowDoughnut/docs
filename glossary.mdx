---
title: "Glossary"
---

**Creator**<br />The person who produces the creative work. This is the designer, art director, photographer, videographer, illustrator, copywriter, or other maker who takes the brief and turns it into the actual asset.

**Owner**<br />The one person accountable for a piece of work from start to finish. The owner drives it forward, makes the calls it needs, coordinates anyone else involved, and answers for the result. Ownership is singular: one named person, not a team or a role, so there's never a question of who to ask.

**Peeking**<br />Checking a running test repeatedly and stopping it the moment the result looks significant. Each extra look gives the result another chance to cross the threshold by luck, so stopping on a good day inflates the false-positive rate: the test reports a winner that isn't real. Avoid it by setting the sample size and end date before launch and letting the test run to that plan, whatever the numbers do along the way. Extending is only valid for an external reason (a traffic drop, a tracking break, a seasonal shift), never because the result hasn't reached significance yet.
